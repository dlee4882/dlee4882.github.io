Flybuys Drone Delivery App

Context and Concept Overview

Autonomous vehicles hold an immense amount of potential as an emerging technology which can shape the future and our daily, pragmatic lives. Although still a little far away, multiple advancements have been made by different companies like Amazon and Google to make common autonomous vehicle usage a reality.

Flybuys is a mobile app for an autonomous drone delivery service – A potential solution to the currently difficult and frustrating delivery experiences of apartment residents as urbanisation increases. Flybuys is supported as a third-party payment provider like Paypal and Afterpay, and links directly to the mobile app which can help customize the delivery to the user’s own needs.

User Research

Multiple user research methods were used in order to collect data from a wide range of audiences, which then were narrowed down to key users. Methods included Online Questionnaires, Direct Experience Storyboarding, Observations and semi-structured Interviews. These were specifically chosen to understand context and capture audiences that might online shop more due to internet usage.

My own Affinity Diagram

Research Data was analysed using core methods such as affinity diagramming and grouping key frustrations in order to uncover the core problem statement that needs to be solved. Affinity Diagramming presented us with the final user needs:

1. An efficient and convenient pick-up method

2. A remote confirmation and notification service

3. A necessity to save time for when it is needed most

4. A safe, reliable and secure experience

And in terms of Drones:

- Refined Technology away from Human Error

We ended up with this statement:

"How might we create an apartment delivery experience that is reassuring and convenient for apartment residents?"

Ideation

Different Ideas generated by me

In the concept phase, different drafts and sketches were discussed on how it would solve different problems and tackle the main apartment delivery issues. The concept that was chosen was the autonomous drone technology operating in the existing apartment infrastructure due to the plethora of difficulties faced when dealing with concepts that required an entire change to the apartment infrastructure. The final concept was taken to testing through information architecture and low-fidelity wireframing.

Considering the Information Architecture

First prototype and development consisted of paper prototyping and the use of Marvel App, a low fidelity mock-up application. User testing was focused on flow and user needs, which we tested by giving each participant both flow versions A and B (in different order per participant) then comparing their feedback to what works best. Interviews were based on the Think-Aloud Protocol with post questions asked at the end of the testing for feedback. Users were found through mutual community group members to reduce bias.

Previous Next

A key design decision we made through to the second prototype was choosing the flow. Whether the sign-in is in the payment phase or done in the app was crucial as it would determine how lengthy and how much clarity the final design is going to shape to have.

Second prototype and development consisted of a mid-fidelity wireframe on the Invision App, which had a clearer user interface than the mock-up. We upgraded the user testing so that although the testing would be longer in time per person, we asked constant questions about each page so that participants are guided through their thoughts, being able to speak out any issues more openly. During analysis we discovered 3 main issues and requests from almost all our participants. These were:

1. Condense the Process

2. Adding better security and privacy

3. A better way to Sign Off

In order to solve these issues, we were looking at removing the next buttons, which would cut down on the process by a half, resolve to a confirmation feedback page at the end and a clear and animation-incorporated sign-off page which would help determine the live movements of the drone.

8. Overall Reflection

I approached this entire unit with a heap of hopelessness, because I was very uncomfortable doing any web/app design or wireframing. Although they are my biggest weaknesses, I am still relieved that I had a group that was supportive, and that I was able to perform my strengths during the entire process. I was always down for analysis and research engagement, finding participants for as many testing phases as we needed. I was also invested into creating animations and transitions, as well as anything to do with digital media to balance the workload. I still wish that I had picked up web design and/or Sketch/Framer earlier, but either way I don’t know how I would have coped since I was always working on windows-based systems.

Overall I think that this was a rewarding unit in terms of working as a team, but was personally a struggle since it was my weakest skills that I was being assessed on.
